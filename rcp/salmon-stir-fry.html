This was a dish I originally created for my girlfriend's birthday lunch - she likes salmon, and she likes stir-fry, so it was a no-brainer. I made some slight changes this time around, and unfortunately it didn't quite live up to how good it was the first time, but that's probably nostalgia and sleep deprivation speaking...

The wok-hei from the stir-fry technique makes the veg and noodles really stand out, and brings out the best in the fermented sauces typical in East-Asian cooking. The grilled salmon is the stand-out centrepiece of this dish, though, with the smoked paprika adding a subtle twang amidst the decadent sweet and umami flavours of the glaze.

Ingredients

Serves four

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • Light soy sauce
  • Balsamic vinegar
  • Honey
  • Smoked paprika
  • 4 Nests of egg noodles
  • 1 Medium onion
  • 2 Cloves of garlic
  • Fresh ginger
  • 2 Thai red chillis
  • 1 Leek
  • 2 Carrots
  • Oyster sauce
  • Dark soy sauce
  • Fish sauce

Method

  1. Rub around a teaspoon each of light soy sauce, balsamic vinegar, honey, and smoked paprika into each of the salmon fillets, then leave in the fridge for 20-30 minutes to marinate. With salmon, it is important not to marinate for too long, as this can denature the fish and make the texture mushy. That being said, the first time I made this recipe, I marinated the salmon for around four hours with no negative effects, so your mileage may vary.
  2. Preheat the grill on a medium heat around 10 minutes before marination is due to be done.
  3. About 5 minutes before marination is due to finish, start preparing the noodles. Place all four nests in a wok and cover with boiling water, turning the heat to medium. Cook for rougly 3-4 minutes.
  4. While the noodles are boiling, start preparing the aromatics: slice the onion, grate the garlic and ginger, and finely mince the two chillis.
  5. Once the noodles are finished, drain into a collinder or sieve and rinse with cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.
  6. At this point, transfer the salmon to a baking tray/grill and pour on half of any remaining marinade. Save the rest for later.
  7. Finish any further preparation of aromatics and heat the wok over a low heat to dry it. Once dry, turn the heat up to high and add in a splash of oil, swirling it around the pan to create a non-stick coating. Add the aromatics and turn the heat down to medium.
  8. Peel and slice the carrot into thin rectangles, adding to the wok. Half the leek and wash thouroughly under running water - dirt has a tendancy to collect in a diagonal line where the green meets the white. Slice and add to the wok.
  9. Once the vegetables have softened slightly, add the noodles and toss to avoid sticking.
  10. Add in the leftover marinade, two tablespoons of oyster sauce, a tablespoon each of light and dark soy sauce, a tablespoon of honey, and a small squirt of fish sauce. Toss everything together and turn the heat up to light any aerosolised oil particles on fire, creating a smokey flavour known as "wok-hei". N.B - this only works on gas stoves.
  11. Plate up your portions of stir-fry and remove the salmon from the grill, laying one fillet on top of each plate.The salmon should have a nice char on the top by this point, and should flake easily under a fork.
  12. Grab some chopsticks (or a fork, you heathen) and dig in.
